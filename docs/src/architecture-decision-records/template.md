# ADR#XXX Title

## Topic

Describe the decision you want to make.  
What is the technical or architectural concern?

Write clearly and briefly.

## Problem

Explain:
- Why this decision is needed
- What issue or limitation currently exists
- What risks may happen if nothing changes

Focus on context, not the solution.

## Options

List the possible solutions you are considering.

For each option, describe:
- What the solution is
- Its advantages
- Its disadvantages or trade-offs

Include at least one alternative besides your preferred choice.

## Decision

State which option you choose.

Be clear and direct:
“We choose Option X.”

## Reasoning

Explain why you selected this option.

Answer:
- Why is this better than the alternatives?
- What trade-offs are accepted?
- How does this align with project goals?

## Discussion History

When creating ADR guidelines, the Discussion History section is not the decision itself. Instead, it records how the team arrived at the decision.

**It serves as a lightweight audit trail so future readers can understand:**

- When the discussion happened
- Who raised the concern (optional)
- What alternatives were considered
- How the decision evolved over time
- Whether previous assumptions or decisions were changed

The goal is to preserve important context that may otherwise be lost in meeting notes, chat messages, emails, or issue trackers.

**What to Include**
- Date of discussion
- Key concerns or problems raised
- Important findings or discoveries
- Significant changes in direction
- Agreements reached during discussions

**What Not to Include**
- Detailed meeting minutes
- Every comment from every participant
- Repetition of information already documented in the Problem, Options, or Decision sections
- Unrelated discussions
